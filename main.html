A Glimpse into Modern Architecture

Notable Architectural Styles

Visual Description Visual

**Gothic Architecture** emerged in the High Middle Ages. It is known for its **pointed arches**, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses, which allowed for thinner walls and vast stained-glass windows. Iconic examples include the Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris and the Westminster Abbey.

**Baroque Architecture** originated in 16th-century Italy. It is characterized by **opulence, dramatic effects**, and a sense of movement, often employing domes, sweeping curves, and rich interior decoration. St. Peter's Basilica in Rome is a prime example of this style.
